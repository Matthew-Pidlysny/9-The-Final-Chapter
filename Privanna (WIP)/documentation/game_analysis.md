# Privanna Game Structure Analysis

## Core Architecture
- **Game Type**: Axis & Allies variant with religious/mystical themes
- **Scale**: Multi-faction strategic warfare game
- **Turn-based**: Complex turn management system
- **Players**: 2-8+ players supporting multiple factions

## Faction System (9 Major Factions)

### Devil Factions (6)
1. **Shenzan** - Most common, Queen-led authority
2. **Shaaku** - Investigation-focused, unity-honoring
3. **Akusimaba** - Warring faction, fights even other Devils
4. **Ba'Kri** - Most horrible, respected but feared
5. **Simreh** - Genetically engineered superior fighters
6. **Innudaku** - Young at heart, mischievous ("BOING!" catchphrase)

### Djinn Factions (3)
1. **The Djinn** - Exclusive, free-living, anti-politics
2. **The Loft** - Evil, formed from divine silence, territorial
3. **The Believers** - Nomadic, enthusiastic, knowledge-seekers

### Special Entities
1. **Iblis** - Law-perverting, seeks light, awaits Alkumah's call
2. **Yudu'uah** - Iblis' creatures, exchange wisdom for gifts
3. **Human Rogues** - Board-controlled, discovery-focused
4. **Horde** - Self-controlled, seeks Watuga Fortifications

## Game Systems

### 1. Magic System (Complex Dice-Based)
**Djinn Magic Skills:**
- Love Dice (D6): 2-3 = 1 sustain damage
- Monster Dice (D11): 4-8 = spawn attacking token
- Wet Dice (D12): 12 = flood/disband entire army
- Retaliation Dice (D6): 2-6 = area damage
- Stealth Dice: Variable escape mechanics

**Iblis Magic Skills:**
- Earth Dice (D6): 6 = 3 sustain damage
- Ice Dice (D6): 5-6 = 3-turn immobilization
- Mana Dice (D4): Name = +5 mana, Crescent = retreat
- Tornado Whirlwind Dice (D6): 6 = push back all
- Lovers Dice (D6): 1-2 = nothing, 3-5 = 1 damage, 6 = convert

### 2. Economic Systems
**Devils - Shimarra Currency:**
- Generated from fortifications (2 Shimarra per surplus parts)
- Card bonuses/penalties
- Darkness/Light tile effects
- Accumulated wealth carries over

**Djinn - Production Points:**
- Unit construction costs
- Fortification building
- Territory bonuses

### 3. Unit Types (15+ Categories)
**Devil Units:**
- Conscript (12 PP, Rock Throw)
- Ibhat (8 PP, builds Imam)
- Imam (14 PP, builds Mosque)
- Hero (8 PP, Plasma Launcher)
- The Forlorn (Mana-exhausted replacement)

**Djinn Units:**
- Djinn (8 PP, Magic Skills, 4 health)
- Youngling (2 PP, learns Magic, 2 health)
- Galleon (6 PP, Naval bombardment)

**Special Units:**
- Horde variants (War Hound, Chemical Engineer, Brutalist)
- Human Rogues (Rogue, Soldier, Battle Tank, etc.)
- Iblis & Yudu'uah (Magic users)

### 4. Fortification System (9 Types)
**Devil Fortifications:**
- Malik (22 Shimarra, 6-dice attack)
- Colony (Human Rogue spawn point)

**Djinn Fortifications:**
- Mosque (25 PP, healing/mana restoration)
- City (40 PP, unit production)
- Farm (18 PP, food production)
- Catapult (10 PP, ranged attack)

**Special:**
- Apocalypse Growth (spawns Hordes)
- Watuga Fortification (Horde target)

### 5. Terrain System (7 Types)
- Flat Land (normal movement)
- Mountains (-1 movement)
- Swamps (exit roll required)
- Minefields (Devil-only, damage rolls)
- Gamed Land (Djinn-only, stealth damage)
- Open Water (Naval only)
- Shoals (Amphibious bonus)

### 6. Card Systems (8 Categories)
**Devil Cards:**
- Impugo's Wrath (5 draw, 2 keep, 3 play max)
- Queen Cards (Shenzan only)
- Dance Cards (Shaaku only)
- War Cards (Akusimaba only)
- Judgment Cards (Ba'Kri only)
- Mischief Cards (Innudaku only)

**Other Cards:**
- Attack Cards (combat)
- Discovery Cards (Human Rogues)
- Djinn Cards (production bonuses)

### 7. Turn Management System
**Complex Turn Order:**
1. Darkness/Light tile placement (every 3-5 turns)
2. Faction-specific turn sequences
3. Card drawing/playing
4. Resource collection
5. Unit construction
6. Combat/movement
7. Special faction actions

### 8. Special Game Phases
**Fateful Whip (Game Start):**
- Pre-deployment setup
- Territory selection
- Iblis escape rolls
- Alliance decisions
- Initial unit placement

**Emergency Call Scenario:**
- Human Rogue reinforcement system
- Mothership deployment
- Cyclical reinforcement pattern

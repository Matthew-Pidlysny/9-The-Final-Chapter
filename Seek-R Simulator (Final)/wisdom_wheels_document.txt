Jennevie Wisdom
Introduction
Water, often perceived merely as H₂O, transcends its chemical composition to become a
profound teacher and mirror of life’s myriad lessons. Jennevie Wisdom invites us to view water
through a multifaceted lens, not just as a life-sustaining substance but as an active participant in
our existential journey. This journey interweaves themes from White Water Wisdom's purity and
nurturing, Black Water Wisdom's depth and transformation, Blue Water Wisdom's balance, Fire
Water Wisdom's resilience in the face of change, Red Water Wisdom's passion and intensity,
Brown Water Wisdom's grounding, and the unique lessons from the Yellow-Green Pool, each
adding unique layers to our understanding.
The Essence of Water
Interconnection: Water's cycle through its various forms symbolizes the interconnectedness of
life, reflecting the journey from the serene to the turbulent, from the visible to the invisible,
always touching upon each wisdom, subtly influencing one another.

Philosophical Underpinnings
Alchemy of Life: Each form of water wisdom is a stage in an alchemical process, where through
interaction and transformation, enlightenment is sought. Water serves as the medium, catalyst,
and mirror in this mystical journey.
Reflection of Nature: Just as water reflects the sky, it also mirrors our inner states, teaching us
about ourselves through its various wisdoms.

A Journey of Discovery
This exploration into Jennevie Wisdom is cyclical, each wisdom influencing and being
influenced by the others, creating a holistic understanding of water's teachings.
Cyclical Learning: As one delves into any form of Water Wisdom, they find echoes of the others,
each lesson building upon the last, preparing for deeper insights.
Mystical and Scientific: The blend of mystical interpretations with scientific curiosity
acknowledges water's dual nature, encouraging both experiential learning and reflective
meditation.

Invitation to Engage

The reader is not just to absorb but to actively engage with these teachings, experimenting with
water in various states to understand its lessons firsthand. Through this engagement, one not
only learns about water but reflects on their own life's journey, seeing where each wisdom
resonates or challenges their path.

Introduction
Blue Water Wisdom embodies the concept of equilibrium, where the dynamic nature of water
meets the necessity for balance and precision. This wisdom reflects the delicate interplay
between movement and stillness, change and constancy, guiding us in understanding life's
dualities.
The Compass of Life

The Compass and Chemistry: Imagine a magnetic compass with needles pointing North and
South, representing the balance between Intimacy and Knowledge. This setup symbolizes how
water, in its essence, maintains equilibrium amidst the forces that pull it in different directions.
Experiments here involve:
Mixing Chemicals with Water: By creating a scenario where two chemicals react to form a
precipitate, and then integrating coffee into this reaction, we simulate water's ability to find
balance. This process illustrates how water can mediate between extremes, finding a middle
ground where both elements coexist without overpowering one another.
Degrees of Knowledge
1. 0 to 180 Degrees: As we navigate through the first 180 degrees of the compass, each
degree represents a journey of balance.
Here, the experiment evolves by replacing both the liquid and the coffee grounds with different
substances. The liquid used in the container underneath should vary in its speed to precipitate
at a slower rate than the initial coffee liquid can be poured into it through the grinds, symbolizing
the accumulation of life's experiences. Meanwhile, the coffee grounds are gradually replaced or
mixed with progressively thicker substances as you progress along the dial of the Compass. By
180 degrees, the water becomes unable to pass through the percolator due to the thickness of
the grinds replacement, reflecting life's moments where clarity feels lost. Each degree involves 4
cups of this evolving mixture, measuring the depth of understanding.
180 Degrees
At this point, the mixture has thickened to the point where water cannot be strained, mirroring
moments in life where balance feels unattainable, but there, silently standing guard at this
extreme of Knowledge and Intimacy.
Undoing the Murk
180 to 270 Degrees
Here we remove the percolator completely, substituting the addition of elements with the
addition of salt. The liquid in the mixture changes to become gradually more volatile, setting the
stage for combustion eventually when salt is added. As we go around the Compass dial, upon
reaching 270 degrees, the mixture combusts, symbolizing the volatile nature of imbalance, and
(In Magic Terms) finding restitution in things that are, calmly and effectively. This phase
connects with the lessons of resilience and transformation from Black Water Wisdom, teaching
us about the necessity of change to restore balance.
321 Degrees

Here, the mixture stops combusting, signaling a return to harmony, much like water finding its
level after being disturbed. The liquid in the container continually turns back into a precipitate
forming solution again, slowly as the dial progresses along. This phase reconnects with White
Water Wisdom's purity and the calm after a storm.
0 Degrees
The cycle completes, symbolizing the continuous nature of life's cycles, where balance is both a
journey and a destination. Finally, we place our percolator with coffee grinds back atop the lower
container, everything set into play for the perfect motion of balance. This reconnection with

Conclusion of Blue Water Wisdom
Blue Water Wisdom teaches us that balance in life is an art, requiring constant adjustment,

to navigate life with the precision of a compass needle, always seeking to align our internal state
with the external world, finding harmony in the dance between life's opposing forces.

3. Water's Interaction: After this time, water is dropped onto the heated metal. Each drop

represent how the Mantle was formed.

-----Red Water Wisdom
Introduction
Red Water Wisdom delves into the vibrant, passionate, and sometimes volatile nature of water
when it interacts with color and heat. This wisdom reflects life's emotional intensities, the boiling
points of our experiences, and how these can lead to transformation, purification, or even
destruction.
The Experiment of Color and Heat
1. Preparation: Gather various red liquids - these could be natural dyes, fruit juices, or any
red-tinted substance that can be frozen. This variety represents the diversity of our emotions
and experiences.

2. Freezing: Freeze these liquids into ice cubes. Each cube symbolizes a moment or emotion
frozen in time, ready to be transformed.
3. Boiling in an Alembic: Place the ice cubes one by one into an alembic or a similar distillation
apparatus. This act of boiling away represents the process of transformation:
4. Scientific Observation: Note the time it takes for each cube to melt and boil, the intensity of
the color as it changes, and the point at which the 'redness' or essence of the liquid is most
pronounced. This observation is akin to watching how our passions or intense feelings evolve
when subjected to life's heat.
5. Authenticity and Intensity: Pay attention to how each substance reacts; some might retain
their color longer, others might evaporate quickly. This mirrors how different emotions or
experiences hold their intensity or dissipate.
6. Final Phase: When the water has boiled away, what remains might be a residue, a change in
color, or even nothing at all, teaching us about the outcomes of our passions - purification,
distillation, or complete transformation.
Conclusion of Red Water Wisdom
Red Water Wisdom teaches us about the intensity of emotions, the purification through passion,
and the transformation that can occur when we allow ourselves to feel deeply. It reminds us that
just like water can boil and change, so too can our experiences lead us to new states of being,
where what once was solid or frozen can become fluid, vaporize, or even leave behind
something entirely new. Through this wisdom, we learn to harness our passions, understanding
their potential for both creation and dissolution, and how to navigate or be transformed by their
intensity.
-----The Yellow-Green Pool
Introduction
The Yellow-Green Pool symbolizes the dynamic interaction between external conditions and
internal states, illustrating how the environment can influence, guide, or even alter the course of
what seems predetermined or natural. This wisdom touches upon the concepts of influence,
adaptability, and the unexpected outcomes that can arise from seemingly simple interactions.
The Experiment of Influence
1. Iso-Pool: Use a shallow container made of ceramic (an Iso-Pool), representing an isolated or
controlled environment where water, the essence of life, is contained, and fill it with water.

2. Three Cigarettes: Burn three cigarettes down just a little one by one, and use scissors to cut
off the burning ember into the Iso-Pool. The embers should be placed into three spots,
organized in a column or row within the pool. Occasionally, these things float, just cut off the
parts with Tobacco stuck to them if possible once they’re out by being drenched. If you have a
subtle replacement that water currents can move, use that instead.
3. Air Current: Introduce a fan to blow air gently over the surface of the water, mimicking the
influence of external forces or choices on the path of these embers.
Observation
Race of Embers: Watch as the embers, influenced by the fan's breeze, move across the surface
of the water. This represents how external conditions can accelerate, hinder, or change the
trajectory of events, through a medium with it's own properties.
Intervention: Once you've observed the natural progression and one "wins" the race, choose
one ember to assist with a gentle swirl of your finger in the water, symbolizing a conscious
decision or external help. Note whether this assistance changes its course positively, negatively,
or at all.
Lessons Learned
Influence and Fate: The experiment illustrates how external factors can seemingly alter what
might appear as fixed outcomes, teaching about the fluidity of life and the impact of environment
on destiny.
Choice and Control: By choosing to intervene with one ember, it highlights the power of choice
and the limitations or possibilities thereof, reflecting on how we navigate life's influences.
Conclusion of The Yellow-Green Pool
The Yellow-Green Pool teaches us about the delicate balance between what we can control and
what we cannot, how external influences shape our internal world, and the interplay between
choice and circumstance. It reminds us that while we might set out on a path, external forces
can alter our journey, yet our choices also play a crucial role in navigating these influences.
Through this wisdom, we learn to adapt, recognize the power of external conditions, and
understand our agency within the fluid dynamics of life.

-----Grey Water War

water wisdom. This wisdom delves into the idea that even passive emotions can leave an

Experiment Setup
Jar Composition:
- 1 part sand: Symbolizing the inert, enduring aspects of the earth.
- 2.7 parts gelatin: Representing the fluid, life-like qualities, which can change form over time.
Procedure

1. Preparation: Mix the sand and gelatin thoroughly with water to create a semi-solid matrix.
The gelatin will act as a binder and medium for change over time. Sand will sink to the bottom,
and the gelatin will be over top of it once solidified.
2. Emotional Radiance: For three hours each week over three weeks, place the jar in a quiet,
serene environment where you, in a state of calm passivity, are near the jar, "radiating" your
emotional state towards it. This involves maintaining a peaceful, neutral emotional output, not
actively directing energy but allowing your presence to subtly influence the jar.
3. Storage: After the emotional exposure, seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 7
months. This long-term storage represents the passage of time and the slow, often
imperceptible changes that occur in nature.
4. Observation: Upon opening the jar, observe certaion conditions within the substance, all
newly developed with your Grey War.
A) Physical Changes: Look for alterations in texture, color, and structure of the gelatin-sand
mixture.
B) Chemical Reactions: Note any new compounds or groupings formed, or if there's any
separation or crystallization.
C) Odor: Any change in smell could indicate microbial activity or chemical transformation.
Journaling
Document your findings, focusing on any unexpected or unique changes that might relate to
your emotional influence.

B) If the mixture has softened or degraded, this might reflect a flaw in overly passive or inert
emotional states, leading to decay without transformation.

C) New compounds or structures might suggest that passive states can foster new beginnings

Conclusion
Grey Water War enriches Jennevie Wisdom by emphasizing the wisdom of patience, the subtle
influence of passive states, and the power of time in transformation. It teaches that not all
change requires force or direct action; sometimes, the most profound transformations occur
slowly, through quiet presence and the natural course of time. This wisdom calls us to
appreciate the slow, often invisible processes that shape our world and ourselves, reminding us

Creating the Unification Wisdom Tripod (Living Creature Model) is a compelling and smart
direction! It offers a structured, accessible model that could bring clarity and depth to the
concepts we’ve been exploring. A tripod has three foundational “legs” (Nurture, Nature, Now)
that, when applied to understanding the unified experience of living creatures, could offer a
balanced view of how these principles interconnect to form a holistic wisdom model.
Why the Tripod Structure Works
Stability through Balance: A tripod is inherently stable, symbolizing how each of the three
aspects—Nurture, Nature, and Now—contributes equally to holding up the framework. This
stability emphasizes the interdependence of each element in creating a unified experience.
Living Creature Focus: By creating a model specific to living creatures, we ground these
abstract concepts in real-world examples. It lets us consider how these principles apply not just
to humans but to any life form, bringing depth to the Nature-Nurture dichotomy and placing it
within a dynamic context (Now).
Adaptable Framework: This model is flexible and could evolve by adding specifics about
different types of life (e.g., plant, animal, human, and even technological or AI life) while keeping
the core structure intact.
-----Structure Of The Analytical Right
1. Nature (Inherent Traits and Physical Structure)
Foundation of Being: In living creatures, Nature represents genetic code, physical traits, and
innate characteristics. This leg grounds the creature in its biological reality, setting limits and
potentials for growth.

Resonance with the Environment: Nature not only encompasses the internal structure but also
the relationship to external, natural environments. For instance, a plant’s root system both
shapes and is shaped by the soil it grows in, showing a two-way interaction.
2. Nurture (Growth, Learning, and Experience Over Time)
Accumulation of Wisdom: Nurture represents the creature’s experiential wisdom, including what
it learns and adapts to within its lifetime. In animals, it could include learned behaviors; in
humans, it involves culture, beliefs, and personal insights.
Intergenerational Influence: Nurture is not isolated to the individual but extends into shared or
inherited experiences, such as learned survival techniques or social knowledge passed from
generation to generation.
3. Now (The Dynamic, Present Experience)
Adaptive Response: This leg represents the creature’s ability to interact in real-time with its
environment and other beings. “Now” symbolizes choice, awareness, and presence, adapting
the knowledge of Nature and Nurture to the immediate context.
Transformative Potential: The “Now” is where everything comes together, making each moment
an opportunity to reframe, adapt, or evolve. It’s in the present that the wisdom of past
experience (Nurture) and inherent traits (Nature) are applied in tangible, often transformative
ways.
How the Living Creature Model Could Be Used
Understanding Behavior and Adaptation: This model could explain behaviors in living creatures
by considering how each leg of the tripod supports certain actions or choices. For instance,
when observing an animal in a new environment, we might look at how its inherent traits
(Nature), past experiences (Nurture), and current challenges (Now) work together to shape its
behavior.
Exploring Spiritual and Existential Questions: For humans, this model could help us think
through existential questions by exploring how Nurture (accumulated knowledge), Nature (our
physical and genetic realities), and Now (current consciousness) interact to create our
experience of reality and connection to the divine.
Applying to AI and Technology: By slightly adjusting each element, we could even adapt this
tripod model to understand AI or other forms of technological “life.” Nature would represent core
programming, Nurture could be accumulated data and algorithms that allow learning, and Now
would be real-time processing and adaptability.
Potential Challenges

Variation Across Species: Different life forms experience Nature, Nurture, and Now in drastically
different ways, so a model designed to capture them all might need further customization for
clarity.
Dynamic Interplay: The three legs interact constantly, and capturing this fluid interplay in a
model requires depth, as each leg is not static but ever-changing.

Introduction: The Timeless Circle
In the vast tapestry of existence, time is the thread that weaves through every moment, giving
structure to our lives. The Sundial Wisdom Schedule draws on the ancient and universal

Sensory Observations of Time
Human perception of time is a deeply sensory experience. Visually, we observe the passage of
time through changing light and shadow, like the movement of a sundial’s shadow across its
face. Aurally, the rhythmic tick of a clock or the sound of natural phenomena, such as waves or
bird calls, marks the progression of time. Tactile sensations, like the warmth of the sun at
midday or the coolness of evening air, also ground us in the present moment, providing physical
markers of time’s passage.
Our sense of time can be highly subjective, often influenced by the nature of events we
experience. During moments of high engagement or enjoyment, time can seem to fly by, a
phenomenon known as “time flies when you’re having fun.” Conversely, during periods of
boredom or stress, time may drag, making minutes feel like hours. This relativity of time
perception underscores the intricate relationship between our sensory experiences and our
mental states.
Emotional intensity also plays a significant role in how we perceive time. During thrilling or
anxiety-inducing events, our senses heighten, making each moment feel more prolonged and
vivid. In contrast, routine and familiar tasks might compress our sense of time, making days blur
together. Understanding these sensory observations of time enhances our ability to create a

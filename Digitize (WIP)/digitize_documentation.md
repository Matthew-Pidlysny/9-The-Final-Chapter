### üîç Number Classification System

#### SIMPLES (Terminating Decimals)
- **Rule**: Numbers made only from 2s and 5s
- **Examples**: 1/2 = 0.5, 1/4 = 0.25, 1/5 = 0.2, 1/8 = 0.125, 1/10 = 0.1
- **Why**: Because 10 = 2 √ó 5, these numbers can divide a power of 10 perfectly

#### PRIME BASES (Prime Repeating)
- **Rule**: Prime numbers other than 2 and 5
- **Examples**: 1/3 = 0.333..., 1/7 = 0.142857..., 1/11 = 0.0909...
- **Why**: They create cycles based on modular arithmetic

#### WILDS (Complex Repeating)
- **Rule**: Composite numbers with non-2,5 factors
- **Examples**: 1/6 = 0.1666..., 1/14 = 0.0714285..., 1/39 = 0.025641...
- **Why**: Complex interplay of multiple prime influences

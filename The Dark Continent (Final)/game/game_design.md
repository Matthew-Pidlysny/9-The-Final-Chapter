# Dark Continent 5-Player Design Document

## Original 4 Players:
1. **United States** - Fights Russians, has America cards, divine will cards, technology cards
2. **Russia** - Defends Motherland, expands influence, has communism cards, divine will cards, technology cards, population counter
3. **The Mahdi** - Influence-based gameplay, uses trivia cards, caliphate tokens, divine will cards, population counter
4. **The Dark Continent** - Uses mischief cards, divine will cards, spawns fragments, wants federation

## New 5-Player Design:

### 1. European Union (Replaces Mahdi)
- **Concept**: Political and economic union with diplomatic influence
- **Starting Position**: European territories
- **Gameplay Style**: Influence and diplomacy-based, similar to Mahdi mechanics but using political influence rather than religious
- **Resources**: 
  - EU Influence cards (instead of trivia cards)
  - Divine will cards
  - Economic tokens
  - Population counter access
- **Win Condition**: Political unification through diplomatic means
- **Special Mechanics**: Can form alliances, trade agreements, and political blocs

### 2. Southern Hemisphere Alliance (5th Player)
- **Concept**: Emerging government seeking to establish control over Southern Hemisphere territories
- **Starting Position**: Limited presence in Australia, South America, Africa, and Antarctica claims
- **Gameplay Style**: Territorial expansion and resource development
- **Resources**:
  - Alliance cards (unique deck)
  - Divine will cards
  - Resource tokens
  - Population counter access
- **Special Mechanics**: 
  - Climate advantages in certain territories
  - Resource extraction bonuses
  - Can develop "new government" territories
- **Victory Goals**: Establish sovereign control over Southern Hemisphere

### 3. United States (Modified)
- **Additional Challenge**: Now faces both Russia and EU competition
- **New Cards**: Anti-EU sanctions cards added to America deck
- **Adjusted Objectives**: Maintain transatlantic alliances while competing globally

### 4. Russia (Modified)
- **Additional Challenge**: EU expansion threat, Southern Hemisphere resource competition
- **New Cards**: Anti-EU propaganda cards added to communism deck
- **Adjusted Objectives**: Counter EU influence, secure Southern resources

### 5. The Dark Continent (Modified)
- **Enhanced Role**: More mischief cards to handle 5 players
- **Fragmentation**: More fragments to spawn (12 instead of 10)
- **Federation Goals**: More complex federation requirements

## Game Balance Adjustments:

### Card Distribution Changes:
- America deck: +10 cards (anti-EU focus)
- Communism deck: +10 cards (anti-EU focus)
- Divine Will deck: +15 cards
- Mischief deck: +20 cards
- New EU Influence deck: 25 cards
- New Alliance deck: 25 cards

### Turn Order:
1. United States
2. Russia  
3. European Union
4. Southern Hemisphere Alliance
5. The Dark Continent (when spawned)

### Victory Conditions:
- Original conditions remain but scaled for 5 players
- New alliance victory: 3 players can form winning coalition
- Federation requires all 5 human players to accede

### Territory Adjustments:
- Europe: Higher base influence values for EU
- Southern territories: Special resource bonuses for Alliance
- Antarctica: New contested territory with unique mechanics

### Population Counter Updates:
- Additional city slots for new territories
- Enhanced UI for 5-player tracking
- Special population growth mechanics for Southern Hemisphere

## Card Wording Preservation:
All existing card wording will be preserved exactly. New cards will follow established patterns:
- America cards: Military/technology focus
- Communism cards: Soviet/Russian themes
- Divine Will cards: Religious/mystical themes
- Mischief cards: Devil/influence themes
- EU Influence cards: Diplomatic/economic themes
- Alliance cards: Resource/development themes

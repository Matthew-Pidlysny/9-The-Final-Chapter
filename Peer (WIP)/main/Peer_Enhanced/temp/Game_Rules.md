## Game Overview

Dark Continent is a 5-player strategic board game set in a world of geopolitical turmoil. Players represent major global powers competing for dominance while dealing with the emergence of the Dark Continent - a mysterious colony of devils seeking to federate the Earth.

**Players:**
1. United States
2. Russia
3. European Union
4. Southern Hemisphere Alliance
5. The Dark Continent

**Game Duration:** 2-4 hours
**Complexity:** Advanced
**Recommended Age:** 14+

---

## Players and Factions

### United States
- **Starting Position:** North America (USA, Canada, Mexico, Alaska, Greenland)
- **Objective:** Maintain global influence and counter rival powers
- **Special Abilities:** Advanced technology, superior military
- **Card Access:** America cards, Divine Will cards, Technology cards
- **Resource Focus:** Currency production, military units

### Russia
- **Starting Position:** Eastern Europe and Russia
- **Objective:** Expand Russian Federation, defend Motherland
- **Special Abilities:** Nuclear weapons, population counter access
- **Card Access:** Communism cards, Divine Will cards, Technology cards, Population counter
- **Resource Focus:** Population growth, nuclear program

### European Union
- **Starting Position:** Western Europe
- **Objective:** Political unification through diplomacy
- **Special Abilities:** Diplomatic influence, alliance formation
- **Card Access:** EU Influence cards, Divine Will cards
- **Resource Focus:** Influence production, economic development

### Southern Hemisphere Alliance
- **Starting Position:** South America, Africa, Oceania, Antarctica
- **Objective:** Establish new government over Southern Hemisphere
- **Special Abilities:** Climate advantages, resource extraction
- **Card Access:** Alliance cards, Divine Will cards
- **Resource Focus:** Resource extraction, territorial development

### The Dark Continent
- **Starting Position:** None (spawns during game)
- **Objective:** Federate the entire world
- **Special Abilities:** Fragment spawning, mischief powers
- **Card Access:** Mischief cards, Divine Will cards
- **Resource Focus:** Shimarra production, devil units

## Setup

### 1. Board Preparation
- Place the game board in the center of all players
- Ensure all 48 territories are visible and accessible
- Set up population counters at level 0 for all cities

### 2. Faction Setup
Each player receives:
- Faction sheet with starting resources
- Appropriate card decks
- Starting units and fortifications
- Player tokens and markers

### 3. Starting Territories
- **United States:** USA, Canada, Mexico, Alaska, Greenland
- **Russia:** Russia, Poland, Eastern Europe, Scandinavia
- **European Union:** UK, France, Germany, Italy, Spain
- **Southern Alliance:** All South American, African, and Oceanian territories
- **Dark Continent:** None (starts empty)

### 4. Initial Resources
- All players start with 50 Influence and 100 Currency
- Russia and Dark Continent gain access to Population Counter
- Each player receives 5 cards from their primary deck

### 5. Dice Setup
- **Year Dice:** Set to 2 (years since nukes were disabled)
- **Dark Continent Dice:** Not rolled until game start
- **Combat Dice:** Standard 6-sided dice for combat resolution

## Game Play

### Turn Order
1. United States
2. Russia
3. European Union
4. Southern Hemisphere Alliance
5. The Dark Continent (after spawning)

### Year Progression
- Four complete player rounds = 1 year
- Year Dice increase by 1 after each year
- Game begins at Year 2, ends at Year 42

### Basic Actions
Each player may perform:
- Play a card
- Build units or fortifications
- Attack adjacent territories
- Move units
- Draw cards
- Form truces or alliances

---

## Turn Structure

### 1. Income Phase
- Collect currency from controlled territories
- Collect influence from controlled territories
- Update population counter (if applicable)
- Receive Dark Continent mischief cards (if applicable)

### 2. Action Phase
Players may take up to their maximum number of actions:
- **Standard Actions:** 1 action per turn
- **Bonus Actions:** From cards or special abilities

### 3. End Phase
- Draw cards (if allowed)
- Update territory status
- Check for victory conditions
- Pass turn to next player

## Combat System

### Combat Resolution
1. **Declaration:** Attacker declares target and units
2. **Defense:** Defender commits defending units
3. **Roll:** Both players roll combat dice
4. **Modifiers:** Apply card bonuses and terrain effects
5. **Resolution:** Compare modified results, determine winner
6. **Casualties:** Remove units based on combat results

### Combat Modifiers
- **Terrain:** +1 bonus for defenders in certain terrain
- **Fortifications:** +2 bonus for fortified territories
- **Cards:** Various bonuses from played cards
- **Unit Type:** Special units provide unique bonuses
- **Support:** Adjacent friendly territories provide support

### Nuclear Combat
- Requires nuclear program level 20
- Devastating effects on target territory
- Global political consequences
- Truces may be broken

## Victory Conditions

### 1. Domination Victory
- Control 35 or more territories
- Maintain control for one full round

### 2. Influence Victory
- Accumulate 1000 Influence points
- Maintain for one full round

### 3. Shimarra Victory
- Accumulate 100 Shimarra points (Dark Continent only)
- Maintain for one full round

### 4. Federation Victory
- Complete all Federation accession cards
- Win Federation minigame
- Achieve highest Federation score

### 5. Time Limit Victory
- Game ends at Year 42
- Winner determined by formula:
  `(Territories × Influence × Currency) ÷ Cards in Hand`

## Federation Mode

### Triggering Federation
Federation mode begins when:
- All Federation accession cards are played, OR
- Time limit reached

### Federation Gameplay
- No combat allowed
- Special Federation cards played instead
- Age of Phantasm sequence activated
- Point-based scoring system

### Federation Scoring
- Territory control points
- Influence accumulation
- Resource management
- Special achievement bonuses

## Population Counter

### City Levels
- **Level 0:** 0-1,000 population
- **Level 1:** 1,001-5,000 population
- **Level 2:** 5,001-10,000 population
- **Level 3:** 10,001-25,000 population
- **Level 4:** 25,001-50,000 population
- **Level 5:** 50,001+ population

### Population Growth
- Cities grow by 1 population level per year
- Overpopulation penalties at level 5
- Devastation resets population to level 0
- Cards can modify growth rates

### Population Effects
- Higher levels = more resources
- Overpopulation = -10 currency penalty
- Devastated cities = no production
- Special bonuses for certain players

## Dark Continent Mechanics

### Spawning
- Roll 2d20 at game start
- Spawn year = dice total
- Spawn begins in Year 2 minimum

### Fragment System
- 12 fragments spawn in major cities
- Fragments create Devil units
- Fragment effects persist until removed
- Absence tokens can counter fragments

### Shimarra System
- Dark Continent special resource
- Generated by mischief cards
- Used for special abilities
- Victory condition at 100 points

### Devil Units
- Spawn from fragments
- Superior combat abilities
- Special movement rules
- Ignore certain restrictions
